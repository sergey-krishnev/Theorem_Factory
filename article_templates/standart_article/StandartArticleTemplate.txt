\centerline{\bf ON PRODUCTS OF $\mathbb{P}$-SUBNORMAL SUBGROUPS IN FINITE GROUPS} 




\centerline{\bf Summary}

\medskip

A subgroup $H$ of a finite group $G$ is called $\mathbb{P}$-subnormal in $G$
if either $H$ coincides with group $G$ or $H$ can be 
connected with the group $G$ by a chain of subgroups with prime indices. 
If any Sylow subgroup is $\mathbb{P}$-subnormal in a group $G$, then 
$G$ is called a w-supersoluble group.
Properties of $\mathbb{P}$-subnormal subgroups 
 and properties of groups,
which are  a product of its two $\mathbb{P}$-subnormal subgroups, in particular, a product of its
$\mathbb{P}$-subnormal w-supersoluble subgroups have been found.

\medskip

Keywords: finite group, $\Bbb{P}$-subnormal subgroup, w-supersoluble group, 
product of subgroups.
